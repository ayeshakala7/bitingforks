The Best Fluffy Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Yogurt (for serving)
  • 1 mango, peeled and chopped (for serving)
  • Sweet sukkah (a blend of sugar, cinnamon, and cardamom) for sprinkling

Directions:

  • In a bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

  • In a separate bowl, beat the egg, then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Mix well.

  • Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix to keep the pancakes fluffy.

  • Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease with butter or oil.

  • Pour a scoop of batter onto the pan and cook until bubbles form on the surface. Flip and cook for another minute or until golden brown.

  • Serve the pancakes warm with a dollop of yogurt, a sprinkle of sweet sukkah, and a generous topping of fresh mango.

Citrus Panna Cotta

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 2 teaspoons orange zest
  • 1 packet (1 tablespoon) gelatin
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • 2 blood oranges, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 1 normal orange, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 1/4 cup sugar (for soaking the orange slices)

Directions:

  • In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over the cold water and let it sit for 5 minutes to bloom.

  • In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, sugar, vanilla extract, lemon zest, and orange zest. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it begins to steam, but do not let it boil.

  • Once the mixture is hot, remove it from the heat and stir in the bloomed gelatin until fully dissolved.

  • Pour the cream mixture into small cups or molds and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or until fully set.

  • While the panna cotta sets, combine the blood orange and normal orange slices in a bowl. Sprinkle the sugar over the slices and gently toss to coat. Let them sit for about 30 minutes to soak in the sugar.

  • Once the panna cotta has set, remove from the fridge. Carefully unmold the panna cotta by running a knife around the edges if needed.

  • Arrange the orange rings on top of the panna cotta, creating a ring around the dessert, and serve chilled.

Lemon Crumble Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (for the crumble topping)
  • 1/4 cup light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed (for crumble topping)

Directions:

  • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan.

  • In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.

  • In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.

  • Mix in the sour cream, vanilla extract, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Stir until well combined.

  • Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing just until combined.

  • For the crumble topping, in a small bowl, combine the flour and brown sugar. Add the cold cubed butter and rub the mixture together with your fingers until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  • Pour the cake batter into the prepared cake pan. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the top of the cake batter.

  • Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean and the top is golden brown.

  • Remove the cake from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

  • Serve the cake as-is, or dust with powdered sugar for extra sweetness. Enjoy your lemon crumble cake!

Basque Cheesecake

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Butter for greasing the pan
  • 1/2 teaspoon of powdered sugar (optional for dusting)

Directions:

  • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Grease a 9-inch springform pan with butter, lining it with parchment paper to allow for easy removal after baking.

  • In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy using an electric mixer on medium speed.

  • Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the vanilla extract.

  • In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour and salt, then add them to the cream cheese mixture. Mix until fully combined, scraping down the sides as needed.

  • Slowly add the heavy cream while mixing until the batter is smooth and velvety.

  • Pour the batter into the prepared springform pan, smoothing the top with a spatula.

  • Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the center is slightly jiggly. The cheesecake should have a burnt, uneven top, which is characteristic of the Basque style.

  • Remove from the oven and let cool in the pan for 10-15 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

  • Once the cheesecake is completely cooled, remove it from the pan and dust with powdered sugar if desired. Serve at room temperature or chilled.

Yogurt Bowl w/ Pistachio Granola & Berries

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt (or preferred yogurt)
  • 1/4 cup pistachio chunk granola
  • 1/2 cup raspberries
  • 1/2 cup blackberries
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons water (or enough to create a syrup with the sugar)
  • 1-2 tablespoons honey (thick and creamy)

Directions:

  • In a small saucepan, combine the raspberries, blackberries, sugar, and water. Heat over low-medium heat until the berries begin to release their juices and the syrup thickens slightly, about 5 minutes. Stir occasionally, ensuring the berries don't break apart. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.

  • In a bowl, layer the Greek yogurt as the base.

  • Once the berry syrup has cooled slightly, drizzle it over the yogurt, ensuring the berries are evenly distributed across the bowl but not broken.

  • Top the yogurt and berries with pistachio chunk granola, adding a nice crunch to each bite.

  • Finish with a generous dollop of thick honey over the top for extra sweetness and a rich texture.

  • Serve immediately and enjoy your delicious, creamy yogurt bowl with pistachio granola and juicy, syrupy berries.

Fig, Rosemary, and Caramelized Lemon Focaccia with Bocconcini

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (or 2 1/4 teaspoons)
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 6-8 fresh figs, sliced
  • 1 lemon, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon honey (for caramelizing the lemon)
  • 1/2 cup bocconcini (small fresh mozzarella balls)
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Directions:

  • In a bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir to dissolve and let sit for 5-10 minutes, or until the mixture is frothy.

  • Add the flour and salt to the yeast mixture. Stir to combine and form a dough. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic.

  • Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let rise for about 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.

  • While the dough rises, prepare the caramelized lemon: Heat a skillet over medium heat, add the lemon slices, and drizzle with honey. Cook for 5-7 minutes, turning the slices until they become golden brown and caramelized. Set aside to cool.

  • Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Once the dough has risen, punch it down and transfer it to a greased baking sheet or focaccia pan. Gently stretch the dough out to fit the pan.

  • Using your fingers, create dimples all over the surface of the dough. Drizzle olive oil over the top and sprinkle with chopped rosemary.

  • Place the sliced figs and caramelized lemon slices on top of the dough, pressing them lightly into the surface.

  • Bake the focaccia for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through.

  • Once out of the oven, let the focaccia cool slightly. Tear the bocconcini into small pieces and scatter them over the warm focaccia. Drizzle with a little more olive oil before serving.

  • Slice and enjoy your delicious fig, rosemary, and caramelized lemon focaccia with bocconcini!
